Legal glossary

A to Z of legal terms, in plain English
AAnticipatory Bail

A direction to release a person on bail before arrest, sought when someone fears arrest for a non-bailable offence.

CCap Table

A record of who owns what in a company: shares, options, and convertibles, and each holder's ownership percentage.

DDue Diligence

The investigation of a company's legal, financial, and business affairs before an investment or acquisition.

EESOP

Employee Stock Option Plan: a pool of equity set aside to grant employees the option to buy shares over time.

FFEMA

The Foreign Exchange Management Act, which governs foreign investment and cross-border transactions in India.

IIndemnity

A contractual promise to compensate the other party for specified losses or liabilities.

JJurisdiction

The authority of a court to hear and decide a matter, based on subject, territory, or value.

LLiquidation Preference

A term-sheet clause setting who gets paid first, and how much, when a company is sold or wound up.

MMoratorium

A period during insolvency proceedings when legal actions against the company are paused.

PPrecedent

A past judgment that guides or binds later decisions on similar facts and questions of law.

VVesting

The schedule over which a founder or employee earns their equity or options, often over four years.

WWrit Petition

An application to a High Court or the Supreme Court seeking enforcement of fundamental or legal rights.

Landmark judgments timeline

The decisions that shaped Indian law
1973
Kesavananda Bharati v. State of Kerala
The basic structure doctrine: Parliament's power to amend the Constitution cannot destroy its essential features.
(1973) 4 SCC 225 · 13-judge bench
1978
Maneka Gandhi v. Union of India
Expanded Article 21: the right to life and liberty requires fair, just, and reasonable procedure.
(1978) 1 SCC 248
1997
Vishaka v. State of Rajasthan
Judicial guidelines against workplace sexual harassment, later codified in the POSH Act, 2013.
(1997) 6 SCC 241
2017
K.S. Puttaswamy v. Union of India
Recognised privacy as a fundamental right under the Constitution, shaping data protection law.
(2017) 10 SCC 1 · 9-judge bench
2018
Navtej Singh Johar v. Union of India
Decriminalised consensual same-sex relations, reading down Section 377 on grounds of dignity and equality.
(2018) 10 SCC 1
2020
Sushila Aggarwal v. State (NCT of Delhi)
Anticipatory bail need not be time-bound and can extend through trial, subject to conditions.
(2020) 5 SCC 1 · Constitution Bench
