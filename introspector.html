Can a dying declaration alone sustain a conviction under the BNS?
Grounded answer
A dying declaration recorded under Section 26 of the BSA can be the sole basis for conviction, provided the court is satisfied it is voluntary, truthful, and made in a fit mental state1. No corroboration is required as a rule of law, only as a rule of prudence2. Where the declaration is suspicious or incomplete, corroboration becomes essential3.
